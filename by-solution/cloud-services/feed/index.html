CASE STUDY

MOL

Our maritime-specific virtualisation platform delivers high availability across MOL LNG’s entire fleet.

The demands of maritime operations create extraordinary IT challenges. MOL explained, “Our vessels rarely dock, so their IT systems have to be robust and fault tolerant. If something does go wrong, I have to get on a plane and fly to the ship. This is time consuming and costly.”

Navigating complex waters with bespoke maritime IT solutions

When you’re the world leader in LNG transportation, operating over 40 ships that account for approximately 25% of global LNG transport, there’s no room for IT failure. MoLNG needed technology solutions as robust and reliable as its fleet.

Industry:

Maritime transportation & energy

Size:

40+ vessels, 25% of world’s LNG transport

Solution:

Virtualisation solutions, professional services

The challenge

Traditional IT solutions simply don’t work when your infrastructure is constantly moving across the world’s oceans. The company needed a virtualisation solution that could handle the unique maritime environment while maintaining the reliability essential for continuous operations. That’s where we could support.

Having established a strong working relationship through previous collaborations, MoLNG approached us when it needed a new virtualisation solution. The brand knew, because of positive experience working with a dedicated account manager, that we could add great value.

set up a meeting

The solution

Through a series of collaborative meetings with our specialist IT project team, we crafted something truly special – a bespoke solution designed specifically for the demanding maritime environment.

The challenge wasn’t just technical resilience. Space constraints on ships meant we had to be incredibly creative with our approach. Unlike typical virtualisation projects that rely on shared storage devices, we designed a solution using local storage to work within the physical limitations of maritime vessels.

Working with our client from initial discovery to design and full implementation, our team engineered a custom solution based on VMware and HP VSA technology, creating two host VMware systems, each with local storage through the VSAs. This innovative approach delivered the high availability needed for virtual machines while respecting the space and environmental constraints of ocean-going vessels.

The outcome

The implementation transformed MoLNG’s IT infrastructure, providing the robust, fault-tolerant systems they needed for their global operations. The solution eliminated the costly and time-consuming need for emergency flights to ships when IT issues arose.

Our maritime-specific virtualisation platform now provides high availability across their entire fleet, ensuring continuous operations regardless of location. As MoLNG put it: “If you are looking for an IT project services partner that can deliver a truly bespoke solution regardless of environment look no further than Advania.”

CASE STUDY

New College Leicester

Sometimes the biggest challenges create the greatest opportunities for transformation. When New College Leicester experienced a catastrophic server failure that resulted in three weeks of data recovery efforts, it didn’t just want to fix the problem. It wanted to make sure it could never happen again.

Renewed IT resilience in response to a catastrophic failure

New College Leicester is a secondary school and sixth form with approximately 1200 users relying on its IT infrastructure to learn, teach, and succeed.

See how the secondary school and sixth form responded to a catastrophic failure with increased resilience.

Industry:

Education

Size:

800+ students, 500 workstations, 1200 users

Solution:

Storage solutions, data recovery strategy

The challenge

Managing vast amounts of media-rich student data across 500 workstations and supporting 1200 users, plus peripheral functions like websites, door security, and CCTV, the college generates enormous data volumes that demand both performance and scalability.

Naturally, the failure was devastating in its scope and impact. As Iain Fallon, IT Manager, explained: “It was a pretty catastrophic failure. The server which went down was old and although we were just about able to source some replacement parts, we went through three new drives and a raid controller trying to fix it – none of which worked, resulting in a degree of data loss. Any data loss is unacceptable.”

The three-week recovery effort highlighted the vulnerability of New College Leicester’s legacy direct-attached storage system. With a major network upgrade on the horizon, the institution needed a solution that could address immediate resilience needs while positioning it for future growth.

set up a meeting

The solution

Our storage specialist, Lee Smith, brought fresh perspective to the challenge: “New College had been looking at many of the mainstream vendors but the solutions under consideration didn’t deliver as much punch for your pound in my opinion. With one eye on the new upgraded infrastructure, I felt that they could get something more functionally rich that delivered better performance.”

We introduced the college to Nimble Storage – an emerging technology we’d successfully deployed elsewhere. The solution offered compelling advantages: fully hot-swappable components, online monitoring, and the ability to mix flash and traditional SATA disk for optimised performance and capacity.

An online demonstration impressed the college team, and it chose to install the array itself after witnessing its simplicity. We scoped the solution with more than twice the immediate capacity needs, providing substantial room for growth.

set up a meeting

The outcome

The implementation exceeded all expectations. Fallon was amazed by the simplicity: “I couldn’t believe how simple it was. I literally followed the instructions in the box and had the array fully operational in less than half a day.”

The college now operates three daily backup routines covering shared areas, user data, and the School Information Management System (SIMS), yet it’s only using 25% of available capacity. The array initially served as file storage and backup before being repurposed as part of the college’s disaster recovery strategy when the new network was commissioned.

Most importantly, the risk of catastrophic data loss has been eliminated, giving the college confidence in its ability to protect critical student data while supporting continued growth.

Expertise

As five-time winners of the Queen’s Awards for Enterprise, it maintains the highest standards of quality and innovation. When it needed to modernise its IT infrastructure, they chose Advania as its technology partner.

Industry:

Manufacturing

Size:

International family-run business

Solutions:

Data centre & networking

The challenge

Harrison Spinks needed to update its ageing IT estate and modernise its server, storage, and network infrastructure to create a more agile and scalable foundation. As a company that prides itself on honesty, ethical-mindedness, and innovation, it required an IT partner who reflected these same values while providing first-class technical expertise.

Following the initial infrastructure refresh, Harrison Spinks identified the need to build on this foundation and improve business resilience with a comprehensive disaster recovery strategy. Its existing Dell EqualLogic solution was encountering performance and reliability issues, creating potential weak links in its infrastructure that needed addressing.

set up a meeting

We worked closely with Harrison Spinks IT team to convert network, server, and storage systems to the latest HPE technology through a carefully phased refresh that didn’t impact users. For the disaster recovery requirements, instead of simply recommending another expensive 3PAR solution, we proposed HPE Nimble storage, offering 99.9999% reliability at a fraction of the cost. This included comprehensive demos and working with the team to determine the perfect configuration.

A 20TB hybrid-storage array was implemented that blended flash storage and traditional disk, conducting 98% of data reads from SSD while providing ample headroom for future backup requirements. CASE STUDY

HQW Aerospace

With many critical IT functions still being controlled by their former parent company HQW Aerospace essentially needed to build a brand-new infrastructure from scratch to achieve IT independence. 

30% faster infrastructure re-build has huge bearing on independence success

Specialists in the design and manufacture of super precision ball bearings, world leader HQW Aerospace UK (formerly The Barden Corporation) boasts an impressive 70-year heritage supplying state-of-the-art products for demanding environments. Their bearing solutions are critical to medical systems, vacuum technology, and aerospace applications. 

After a former parent company planned to close the Plymouth plant, HQW Aerospace was acquired by precision bearing manufacturer HQW Precision GmbH. HQW now faced the immediate challenge of building a brand-new IT infrastructure to operate independently within a tight 6-month deadline. 

Industry:

Manufacturing

Organisation size:

300+ employees

Solutions:

Cloud solutions, data centre & networking

The challenge

The acquisition presented a 6-month deadline to retire existing off-site systems and ensure the necessary on-premises infrastructure was in place to allow their IT to fully return to the UK. This involved orchestrating the repatriation of the business’ entire data archive from Germany to HQW’s Plymouth site, which would need new infrastructure to host and secure the data. 

For an IT team of only four people, this seemed like a monumental undertaking in such a short timeframe. The new infrastructure would need to be easily managed and highly intelligent, reducing unnecessary burdens on the team whilst ensuring they had the right tools to take back control. 

set up a meeting

The approach

HQW Aerospace UK immediately contacted Advania for expert guidance. Initially keen to make use of legacy Dell equipment to reduce costs, they wanted to identify whether any of their existing technology was still fit-for-purpose. 

Advania conducted a full investigation into the plausibility of harnessing existing infrastructure and orchestrated in-depth workshops to establish what gaps would need to be addressed.  

The investigation revealed that more replacement was needed than originally anticipated, but buying new hardware would turn out to be more cost-effective in the long run. This enabled consideration of modern solutions that would deliver even greater efficiency and manageability. 

set up a meeting

The solution

Advania delivered HQW the comprehensive infrastructure independence they needed, including: 

  • A hyperconverged infrastructure that consolidated compute, storage, and hypervisor, virtualising 40+ physical servers onto a much smaller hardware footprint.
  • Modern wireless network implementation with HPE Aruba solution creating a more cost-effective and easier-to-manage network.
  • Complete data migration from Germany to Plymouth, including recovery of servers thought to have been lost.
  • Extended network deployment to a second site with wireless access points and full SD-WAN solution ensuring warehouse integration with main plant.
  • Technology asset disposal to decommission legacy Dell equipment and recover residual value to offset new hardware costs.


The new infrastructure also enabled HQW Aerospace to operate a more flexible hybrid cloud and extend their resources into various public clouds as required. 

set up a meeting

The outcome

The infrastructure independence project delivered exceptional results:

  • Completed 30% faster than planned with all on-premises infrastructure ready two months before the critical deadline.
  • Achieved substantial cost savings through hardware consolidation, reduced power consumption, and smaller physical footprint.
  • Enabled complete IT self-sufficiency through expert-led training and skills handover ensuring the four-person team could manage the entire estate.
  • Delivered enhanced flexibility through hybrid cloud implementation providing access to collaboration tools and applications anywhere, anytime.
  • Provided free hypervisor through Nutanix Acropolis, avoiding costly VMware licensing still controlled by former parent company.
  • Established ongoing resilience through Monitoring as a Service, Backup as a Service, and on-demand support contracts.


The Nutanix hyperconverged solution successfully consolidated 40 physical servers down to just one, providing colossal space savings, scalability for future growth, and greatly reduced power consumption.

Following the successful infrastructure independence, HQW Aerospace UK is now operating as a fully self-sufficient business alongside HQW Precision GmbH in Germany, backed up with modern, scalable IT infrastructure positioned to support future growth.
