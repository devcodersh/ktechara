CASE STUDY

Domestic & General

Discover how we helped Europe’s leading appliance care provider refresh nearly 2,000 notebooks across five countries, delivering 25% cost savings while solving critical Windows compatibility challenges through innovative AMD technology solution.

Powering productivity across Europe's leading appliance care provider

Sometimes the most complex projects require the most innovative solutions. When Domestic & General needed to refresh notebooks for nearly 2,000 users across five countries, they faced significant compatibility challenges that threatened to derail their timeline. That’s when they turned to Advania – a trusted partner with the vendor relationships and technical knowledge to deliver breakthrough results.

Industry:

Property maintenance

Organisation Size:

16 million customers across 14 countries

Solutions:

Hardware procurement, vendor management, technical consultation delivering modern mobile computing

The challenge

Domestic & General required a complete notebook refresh across nearly 2,000 users spanning five European countries. The project faced several critical considerations that threatened both the timeline and budget.

Having traditionally used Intel®-powered machines, they discovered that Intel’s latest generation would only support Windows 10 operating systems. This presented major application compatibility issues, with nearly 20% of users heavily reliant on mission-critical applications for accounts and complaints that were only compatible with earlier Windows editions.

As Chris Jones, Desktop Services Manager at Domestic & General, explained: “We needed a solution that would deliver predictable performance and breakthrough commercial value without disrupting key applications or forcing immediate application upgrades.”

Additionally, their legacy Intel estate was experiencing nagging firmware issues, resulting in higher-than-normal failure rates – something that needed to be avoided in the new technology choices.

set up a meeting

The solution

Drawing on our established vendor relationships and deep technical expertise, we became Domestic & General’s trusted technology partner for this complex multi-country deployment.

We quickly identified that AMD-based machines could help Domestic & General realise significant economic savings while achieving the same, if not greater, performance versus Intel-equivalent devices. Critically, AMD would enable continued access to their critical applications thanks to wider support for legacy Windows operating systems.

Loyal to the reliable, user-friendly experience they had enjoyed with HP’s first-class range of notebooks, we recommended continuing with HP products but with AMD processors – delivering the perfect balance of familiarity and innovation.

Our pre-sales team’s deep product knowledge proved invaluable throughout the project. We shared technology roadmaps surrounding HP and AMD technology, benchmarked performance data, and introduced demo machines to select user groups. Testing by both standard and high-power users confirmed that AMD’s Accelerated Processing Units delivered welcomed performance improvements.

To overcome supply constraints that were causing unacceptable wait times for new starters, we arranged to hold buffer stock, enabling machines to be called off on demand. We also ensured seamless logistics across all five European sites, with every shipped device being country-specific, localised and ready for deployment.

set up a meeting

The outcome

The refresh has been a resounding success. Switching to AMD-based notebooks delivered comparable performance versus Intel across the board, but with noticeably reduced failure rates and zero impact on the IT team. Most impressively, this was achieved at a cost saving of 25% on similarly configured Intel machines.

Critically, users continue to access all key applications they need, whenever and wherever they want, due to AMD’s compatibility with legacy Windows operating systems. They no longer face the limitations of Windows 10-only devices or pressure for immediate application upgrades.

CASE STUDY

Forum for the Future

With personnel located around the world, Forum for the Future (Forum) had a pressing need to adopt a streamlined data management system and an enhanced technology infrastructure. Ambitious and complex, this data-focused migration would impact every aspect of Forum’s work, enabling staff to work effectively and consistently in a more secure environment. 

Enhancing global data performance

Forum for the Future, a leading international sustainability organisation with offices in the UK, US, India and Singapore work in partnership with business, governments and civil society to accelerate the shift towards a just and regenerative future.

With more than 80 staff worldwide, Forum for the Future (Forum) approached Advania with a pressing need to streamline its data management and enhance its technological infrastructure. Forum needed to augment the capability of its existing IT managed services provider and the Forum in-house IT team with the expertise of Advania’s digital transformation specialists.

Recognising the importance of the work that Forum does, Advania was delighted to partner with a client that not only closely aligns with Advania’s own ESG ambitions but is leading the field in achieving the sustainability goals that will shape the future of business.

Industry:

Not for profit

Organisation Size:

100

Solutions:

Digital employee experience, professional services

Completion date:

April 2024

The challenge

The primary focus for Forum was on migrating its data from Dropbox to SharePoint Online. Both ambitious and technically challenging, this transformative operation would have a big impact on the ability of Forum’s disparate staff to collaborate effectively and consistently, and in a far more secure environment.

Having already successfully migrated much of their IT infrastructure onto the Microsoft 365 platform, Forum was left with a set of data and documents in their legacy Dropbox tenant. Once a key part of their collaboration systems architecture, together with Google Workspace, changes in technology and user needs meant that this platform no longer met user requirements and represented a costly additional expense.

The SharePoint migration required expert assistance from Advania. Forum required a defined target information architecture, together with creating data labels and mapping out their current content. Once its existing SharePoint settings had been reviewed, the user benefits of the new platform required scoping and outlining, before moving to planning the technical migration which would be carried out by the incumbent MSP.

Forum was particularly concerned about the ambition and timing of their plans and looked to Advania for expert validation and guidance throughout this migration project.

set up a meeting

The solution

SharePoint is a versatile platform with many features and capabilities. The key to use it as an effective document and content management platform with an engaging user interface is good design and information architecture.

Leveraging our experience in user-centric information architecture design, our team created a target architecture for the document migration, which introduced a logical and user-friendly structure for Forum’s data. Hosting the data in SharePoint also means that Forum can leverage modern technical capabilities such as Microsoft 365 Copilot.

Our Technical Migration team conducted a migration readiness assessment to evaluate the total size of the data, analyse any migration issues and highlight relevant permission considerations. This thorough assessment laid the groundwork for a smooth transition from Dropbox to SharePoint.

Our team worked in tandem with Forum’s internal IT management and their incumbent managed services provider to structure the migration and oversee its successful delivery.

Throughout the engagement, we maintained open lines of communication with key stakeholders of Forum’s migration team, including the COO and Operations and IT Systems manager, who were pivotal in coordinating the project.

The outcome

With the successful migration from Dropbox to SharePoint Online completed, Forum now benefits from significantly enhanced data management capabilities. The new system offers improved accessibility, better security and streamlined collaboration among team members.

Additionally, the tight timeframe needed by the client was met, meaning that Forum was able to cancel their Dropbox contract in time, securing an early tangible financial benefit of the business case for the migration, ahead of the multitude of others that followed.
