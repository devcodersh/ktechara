Advania, the new name for Content+Cloud, is all in on Copilot for Microsoft 365. We’ve deployed it to all of our UK staff – that’s over 1,000 users – and used our own experience to create a tailored offering for our mutual customers.

How we embraced Copilot for Microsoft 365 

We started early, joining the Early Access Programme (EAP) in October 2023 and getting our hands on Copilot before anyone else. We quickly saw the potential of this artificial intelligence (AI) assistant and decided to run a pilot with 50 of our key users from different teams. We used Microsoft Purview, SharePoint Advanced Management, and our scripts to protect our data and documents and build a solid cyber foundation.

The pilot was a huge success, and we got tons of positive feedback and value from our users. They loved how Copilot helped them work faster, smarter and more creatively. They also came up with some brilliant use cases for Copilot that we could share with our customers. This helped us solidify our decision to roll it out across our whole organisation.

Developing our commercial offering for Copilot for Microsoft 365

But we didn’t stop there. We also used our expertise and knowledge in Microsoft 365 to create a unique offering for Copilot for Microsoft 365. We designed a Copilot Readiness Assessment, a Copilot Adoption Framework and a Copilot Training and Engagement Programme. These are all aimed at helping customers get ready, get started and get the most out of Copilot for Microsoft 365.

And guess what? It worked. We’ve delivered many successful Copilot engagements to our customers, helping them realise the value and benefits of Copilot for Microsoft 365.

We lead with our customer success unit, focused on business change and user adoption, helping customers to run a structured pilot, develop valuable use cases, embed Copilot in their daily activities and mitigate their data risks. We also measure the value and satisfaction of Copilot users and use that data to build a business case and prioritise the deployment for the rest of the organisation.

We think our approach is unique, setting us apart from other Microsoft partners. We don’t just sell Copilot, we help our customers adopt it and love it.

We’re always looking for new ways to improve and innovate with Copilot such as adding more knowledge sources, skills and use cases. We’re also helping customers extend their Copilot experiences with Copilot Studio and adopt the wider Microsoft AI stack, leading with Azure Open AI.

We are also experimenting with Copilot Agents, automating tasks and processes to save users even more time and energy. Some of the use cases we are testing with Copilot Agents are:

  • Generating invoices and sending them to clients automatically
  • Creating and updating project plans and dashboards based on emails and chats
  • Scanning and summarising relevant news and articles for our industry and customers
  • Booking travel and accommodation for our business trips

Leading the way with Copilot for Microsoft 365 in the UK

We’re super proud of our Copilot journey, and we’d love to share our learnings and best practices with you and your customers. We’re committed to being leaders in AI, and we’re grateful for your partnership and support. We want to help you and your customers achieve more with Copilot for Microsoft 365, by providing you with the best solutions, services and support.

Want to know more about our Copilot for Microsoft 365 offering? Check out our webpage.

Do you want to know more about our Copilot story? We are eager to hear from you and explore new ways to collaborate and co-innovate. Contact us today and find out how we can help accelerate your customers’ success with Copilot.

At Advania, we’ve taken a big step towards artificial intelligence (AI) transformation by licensing Copilot for Microsoft 365 across our entire workforce of over 1,000 people in the UK. We’re one of the first major Microsoft partners in the UK to be making this commitment to the AI assistant. Find out how the success of our pilot programme helped us make the decision to go all-in on Copilot for Microsoft 365 in this newsletter exclusive.   

How we embraced Copilot for Microsoft 365 internally

We started our Copilot for Microsoft 365 journey back in October 2023 with the wider Advania group joining the Early Access Programme (EAP), receiving a number of licenses in our Norwegian tenant. This provided some key individuals across the group with early hands-on access to the Copilot technology.

Once Copilot became available through the Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) programme, we immediately invested in 50 Microsoft 365 Copilot licences for our UK production tenant which accelerated our pilot programme.

Working with our management team we identified key pilot users, including consultants delivering Copilot-related services to our customers, plus a variety of user personas from functional teams right across our business. To alleviate any concerns around the historical over-sharing of documents in the business, we also asked our initial pilot group to work to an NDA so they could feel comfortable and enthusiastic about experimenting with prompts on our company data.

During this early adoption stage, we used Microsoft Purview, SharePoint Advanced Management and our own custom scripts to assess and mitigate the sensitive document sharing risk in our tenant.

As we began to see the value that our first cohort of pilot users were getting from Copilot for Microsoft 365, we decided to enrol a further 70 users to the programme just a few months later in March.

Bringing us to present day, the success of this pilot helped us make the decision to license Copilot for Microsoft 365 to all our UK staff, leading us to purchase an additional 1,000 licences last month.

Developing our commercial offering for Copilot for Microsoft 365

As a leading solutions provider for Microsoft Cloud, we were uniquely placed to leverage our expertise, existing knowledge base and services around Microsoft 365 to create a comprehensive and differentiated Copilot for Microsoft 365 offering for our customers. 

We got started by creating a Copilot Readiness Assessment to help customers understand the prerequisites, benefits and risks of using Copilot for Microsoft 365 in their environment. This was then followed by a Copilot Adoption Framework, guiding customers through the stages of planning, deploying, adopting and optimising Copilot for Microsoft 365. After all, in order to realise value from Copilot, users have to change the way that they work which can be a challenge for even the most enthusiastic technology advocate.

Our Copilot training and engagement programme is designed to help our customers get the most value out of their investment in Copilot for Microsoft 365. It helps users master the skills and best practices of using Copilot for Microsoft 365 effectively and responsibly.

Bringing Copilot for Microsoft 365 to our customers

Leveraging the lessons we learned internally, we have delivered many successful Copilot engagements to support customers realising value from their investment in Copilot for Microsoft 365.

Leading with our Adoption and Change Management practice, we help customers to deliver a structured pilot, focusing on developing valuable use cases for Copilot as well as embedding its use in day-to-day activities. Typically in parallel with this early Value Experimentation, we help customers to identify and mitigate their sensitive data sharing risks which are more likely to be surfaced with Copilot.

By working closely with users to get them up-to-speed with Copilot and experiment, not only do we help them to derive value from the technology quickly, but we get to measure that value. What we uncover can be extrapolated to the broader organisation, not only developing a business case founded on use cases specific to the business, but also identifying those users who would benefit most from Copilot, allowing the customer to deliver a phased deployment prioritised by user satisfaction and business value.

Our approach to leverage our technical and change management expertise, and to focus on the business case and return on investment for the customer sets us apart from other Microsoft partners and makes us the partner of choice for the customer in this space.

Leading the way with Copilot for Microsoft 365 in the UK

Licensing Copilot for Microsoft 365 across our entire workforce of over 1,000 has made us one of the first and leading partners in the UK to make such a commitment. We’re proud to be enabling our people to continue delivering on our proven track record of providing value and innovation to our customers with the support of Microsoft’s AI assistant.

Our teams are constantly exploring new ways to extend the reach and capabilities of Copilot for Microsoft 365, adding more knowledge sources, skills and use cases. We’re also experimenting with Copilot Agents, a new feature that allows Copilot to automate tasks and processes asynchronously, saving users even more time and energy.

At Advania, we’re committed to elevating our business leadership in AI by staying ahead of the curve, sharing our learnings and collaborating with partners like Microsoft. This also extends to our commitment to our customers – to help them achieve more with Copilot for Microsoft 365 by providing them with the best solutions, services and support.

Join us on our Copilot for Microsoft 365 journey

You can find out more about the Copilot for Microsoft 365 services we’ve got on offer through our Copilot webpage.

Impressed by our Copilot for Microsoft 365 journey and offering? Spread the word and recommend us to your colleagues, friends, and network. We appreciate your support and referrals.

Each month, our M365 PACE experts take a look at what’s on the cards for Microsoft 365 users. In this blog, we’re sharing our highlights from the new features coming to the Microsoft 365 Roadmap in March.

Microsoft Teams: New Copilot experience in chats

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 180201)

Users with a Copilot for Microsoft 365 licence will be able to use the artificial intelligence (AI) assistant from Teams chats, with the Copilot app being pinned above your chats.

The improved version of the Copilot app offers additional benefits over the app formerly known as Microsoft 365 Chat:

  • Improved prompts with details relevant to you
  • View your Copilot Chat history
  • Access to a library of prompts via Copilot Lab

Microsoft Teams Phone: private line

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 181616)

Microsoft Teams Phone uses are soon going to be able to use a private second phone number available to allow a select set of callers to call them directly, bypassing delegates, admins or assistants.

Private line calls will support incoming calls only, and they will be distinguished by a unique notification and ringtone

Microsoft Viva Pulse: integration into Microsoft Copilot Dashboard from Viva Insights

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 380477)

An update to Microsoft Viva Pulse with allow leaders and IT admins to initiate Copilot impact surveys through Microsoft Viva Pulse, helping them to understand the return on investment of rolling out AI tools like Copilot across their organisation.

Part of the Viva Suite, Viva Pulse allows you to capture sentiment data that maps to work patterns data from Viva Insights to measure AI adoption.

SharePoint: custom document library templates

Microsoft is rolling out an update to SharePoint, allowing you to create custom document library templates. This update leverages the existing PowerShell-based tooling and methods for creating custom list templates.

As a Global Administrator or SharePoint Administrator, you can establish what your organisation needs, specific to your business.

Microsoft Clipchamp: auto-captioning

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 381128) For Clipchamp users, videos can be made more accessible or easy to follow while muted with the latest update bring auto-captioning to videos. You can easily generate real-time captions in one click using the auto-caption feature.

Captions are generated using smart voice detection technology, providing a downloadable SRT file with captions automatically generated with no typing required. The transcript also creates an index of timestamps so you can navigate and edit videos faster. You can easily correct any mistakes in the auto-captions using the captions panel.

Copilot in Forms: create and customise forms

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 375324)

Microsoft is making it faster and easier to create and customise forms with Copilot in Forms, a new feature that lets you use natural language to describe your desired form. Copilot will then create a form that matches your description, allowing you to preview and edit as needed. You can also choose from various themes to elevate your form to make it more engaging for your audience.

Each month, our M365 PACE experts take a look at what’s on the cards for Microsoft 365 users. In this blog, we’re sharing our highlights from the new features coming to the Microsoft 365 Roadmap in.

Microsoft Teams: Intelligent Recap for Copilot users

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 375760)

With the release of Copilot for Microsoft 365 for customers of all sizes on all licence agreements, it’s exciting to see new features coming to the roadmap for the new artificial intelligence (AI) companion.

This month, Copilot users will be able to access intelligent recap after Microsoft Teams meetings to get a summary of the meeting. The summary includes:

  • Personalised timeline markers to easily browse the recordings by when you joined or left, when a screen was shared, and when your name was mentioned
  • Browse recordings by speakers, chapters, and topics
  • Access AI-generated meeting notes, tasks, and jump to name mentions in the transcript

Microsoft Teams: forward chat messages

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 90585)

For all Microsoft Teams users, a new feature is being rolled out now, which allows you to share a message with another colleague by right clicking the message or selecting the three dots and then selecting ‘Forward’.

Not only can you forward messages quickly from one chat to another, but you can also add additional content to the message, providing context and clarity for the recipient. Messages can be forwarded to both 1:1 chats and group chats.

Audio and video flyout and settings in Teams meetings 

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 187098)

Microsoft is adding an audio and video flyout to Teams meetings, making it easier and more efficient for users to manage their audio and video settings during meetings.

You can easily toggle features on and off with visual indicators for both audio and video settings. This means you no longer need to navigate deep into the settings menu to make a quick adjustment. If you didn’t select your background during the pre-join screen, you can easily change it directly from the camera fly-out during the meeting.

Microsoft is also making avatars accessible from the camera flyout, so you can change your video to an avatar in meetings. Other settings include brightness and soft focus of your camera.

Domain activity report for admins

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 370565)

Microsoft is rolling out an external domain activity report in Teams admin centre. The external domain activity report will show Teams administrators which domains their users communicate with. It’s available for both those with open federation on and those with explicit allow lists.

SharePoint Premium: translation 

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 161828)

Microsoft is rolling out a new translation feature in SharePoint Premium, an advanced content management and experiences platform, the next evolution for Syntex.

The translation service enables users to translate documents quickly and accurately among dozens of languages based on specific metadata or on demand. Users will be able to translate files while preserving the original format and structure of the file.

Translation in SharePoint Premium can be completed on different file types either manually or automatically by creating a rule. Custom glossaries and models can also be used to improve the quality and consistency of your translations.

Microsoft Viva: Stories public preview

(Microsoft Roadmap ID 101514)

Microsoft Viva’s Stories are being launched in public preview now, allowing everyone across your organisation to share updates, experiences, vision and perspective  using short videos or photos on their storyline.

Stories are a new way to express yourself authentically among your communities. They can reach and engage people throughout your organisation across Viva Engage, Teams and Outlook.
