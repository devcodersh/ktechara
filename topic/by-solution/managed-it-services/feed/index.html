Discover what Microsoft 365 E7 means for your organisation's AI strategy.

The post Microsoft 365 E7: A new frontier for AI at scale appeared first on Advania UK.

]]>

As organisations mature their AI strategy, the focus is moving beyond experimentation to the strategic integration of AI into key business workflows at scale. This means embedding AI features into the everyday tools users rely on, harnessing its power in day-to-day tasks and actions, and housing AI closer to where business data lives to provide real-world context and intelligence from within your organisation.

The creation of AI agents as focused “digital workers” is also a key part of this shift. But while the potential to simplify workstreams and offload repetitive actions is attractive, the ongoing management and governance of agents creates new identity and administration challenges.

It’s for this reason that Microsoft has released the new Microsoft 365 E7 license, which enters general availability today. Dubbed as “The Frontier Suite”, this new licensing tier (the highest Microsoft 365 tier available) unites core productivity software, AI capabilities, and enterprise security into a single license that streamlines AI adoption and better enables effective governance of AI agents across the organisation.

What’s included with Microsoft 365 E7?

  • Microsoft 365 E5 forms the base, providing the core productivity, security, identity, and compliance foundation that organisations already rely on to work securely at enterprise scale. For organisations that don’t currently utilise an E5 license, moving to M365 E7 represents a powerful upgrade that introduces a valuable portfolio of additional Microsoft tools.
  • Microsoft 365 Copilot adds AI capabilities directly into everyday work experiences. Sitting inside the tools your teams already use every day, it helps users reason, create, and act with context from your organisation’s data. Microsoft Copilot has also recently been upgraded with new functionalities, including the ability to use Anthropic’s Claude models within the platform.
  • Microsoft Entra Suite extends identity and access controls to users, apps, and importantly agents. This means secure access, conditional controls, and least-privilege governance across your entire environment.
  • Agent 365 delivers centralised governance, visibility, and control for agentic AI platforms. This addresses a growing governance gap many organisations face as they deploy AI agents across key workflows, allowing for effective compliance and security monitoring across the organisation, orchestrated by internal teams, or an external Microsoft partner.

With a consolidated pricing structure, an E7 license offers genuine value when compared against the deployment of individual Microsoft components alongside an existing E5 license, while helping organisations more easily upscale features and AI capabilities.

Crucially, E7 should be seen as a standalone licence option rather than simply a step up from E5. With the integration of additional tools and capabilities, it’s Microsoft’s first AI-driven M365 licence tier, shifting AI from a tool that supports individual workers to an organisation-wide capability that acts across systems and processes.

For some, particularly those who have not yet reached the right level of AI maturity, the option of continuing with E5 and extending with additional capability maybe the best short-term approach, as part of a medium to long-term strategy that grows maturity with a view to future E7 deployment.

What is Agent 365, and how does it work with E7?

As agentic AI moves from experimental to operational, effective governance becomes critical. Microsoft-sponsored research from IDC predicts that 1.3 billion agents will be in circulation by 2028. But the transformation enabled by agentic AI also carries new risks. Without proper guardrails in place, rapid deployment of new agents can lead to blind spots, diminished ROI, and real security risks.

It’s for this reason that Agent 365 has been released alongside, and included in, Microsoft 365 E7. A key component of the new license tier, Agent 365 gives IT and security leaders a single place to observe, govern, manage, and secure agents across the organisation. It works alongside existing Microsoft tools for managing users, like Microsoft Admin Centre and the Microsoft Entra Suite, extending management and governance capability within the confines of familiar platforms as part of a more seamless experience.

Agent 365 makes it easier for businesses to take the next step on the AI journey with the shift to agentic deployments, ensuring they can be effectively controlled and managed as genuine ‘digital workers’ alongside human employees. We’ve seen the benefits agentic AI can provide first-hand, as a long-standing Microsoft partner and through our creation of a bespoke agent for handling request-for-proposal (RFP) responses that’s helped one of our customers save over 400 days per year. 

What this means for your AI strategy

Microsoft 365 E7 gives you a path to scale AI confidently across your organisation. The inclusion of Agent 365 means you can extend from AI-assisted productivity into agentic workflows, with governance built in from the start.

With Microsoft 365 Copilot included in the license, users get immediate access to AI across the entirety of the Microsoft productivity suite, building on Microsoft’s Work IQ layer to deliver immediate context from the wider business. Work IQ also integrates into agents deployed within the Microsoft environment and managed with Agent 365, ensuring a consistent user experience between Copilot and bespoke agents.

This allows for a more comprehensive approach to AI, with Copilot providing more generalist support to users across day-to-day workflows, while dedicated agents extend this to provide specific assistance with more structured, technical workflows.

This is the approach we’ve adopted as part of our own AI strategy, enabling users with Copilot first as a generalist tool, then developing bespoke agents for specific workflows. To date, we have a 98% internal adoption rate for Copilot and have built a wide range of agents to support different teams, alongside a number we have subsequently deployed for customers.

What to do next

Whatever the makeup of your existing Microsoft license estate, E7 offers the ideal platform to elevate and evolve your AI strategy. If you’re interested in getting started, get in touch with our team today. As an experienced Microsoft partner, our experts can help you assess your current license estate, review your AI maturity, and help you make informed decisions about how best to deploy Microsoft 365 E7 across your organisation.

The last few weeks have brought some noticeable shifts within the technology supply chain that are impacting the global IT hardware market, including shortages of key components, especially memory products like DRAM and NAND.

This has not only caused limitations in availability but has also extended production timelines and triggered unexpected price adjustments.

What is causing these issues?

This ongoing challenge is driven by a number of factors including emerging supply constraints, rising fabrication costs, and a heightened level of demand to meet the requirement for AI deployments, which typically include more memory-intensive workloads. This has resulted in a sharp spike in demand that key suppliers are working tirelessly to accommodate.

Almost every major manufacturer and distributor has reported challenges with availability of stock, and a sustained increase in price that is now impacting the cost for end customers.

How will this impact our customers?

While it’s not expected that these challenges will lead to outright shortages, customers should expect longer lead times, limitations in available configurations, and incrementally increasing costs. It’s important to note that this situation is not limited to Advania – it is being felt across the IT sector and will likely continue through 2026 and 2027 as the industry realigns to meet this new demand.

For many, the most immediate and visible impact will be higher costs on new hardware compared to previous orders, with more regular price fluctuation than you might be accustomed to. In turn, these uncertainties may require us to provide quotes with shorter validity periods.

Lead times from manufacturers are also becoming less predictable, particularly for highly sought-after models or configurations, causing availability to fluctuate as stock tightens.

These factors combined make effective and proactive IT planning more critical than ever. Customers who engage with us early and have clearly defined roadmaps for their IT can expect greater access to inventory and more predictability in deployment timelines, while more ad-hoc projects may experience additional challenges when sourcing the right hardware in an appropriate timeframe.

How we’re helping our customers

We’re highlighting these challenges as soon as possible to allow more time for strategies to shift as needed, and have already reached out to the majority of customers directly over email. To help alleviate the impact, we’re actively monitoring vendor and distributor pricing, lead times, and availability to help our customers plan ahead. Wherever possible, we’re also leveraging our close relationships with supply chain partners to help prioritise more immediate customer needs. We’ve already seen proactive actions taken by leading vendors such as Cisco, Dell, HPE, HP Inc and Lenovo to help mitigate the impact on customers as much as possible.

In instances where a specific model is delayed or disproportionately affected by price changes, our experts are proactively advising on alternatives that protect timelines and budgets, while working to build flexibility into specifications where appropriate.

We’re also focusing on forward planning for upcoming projects and refresh cycles, helping forecast needs earlier and reduce exposure to volatility. For customers with larger projects on the horizon, or an ongoing refresh cycle, we’d recommend getting in touch with our teams sooner rather than later, so we can help adjust plans to minimise disruption and avoid any unplanned cost adjustments.

If any customers have upcoming hardware requirements or would like to review plans in light of these market conditions, please speak with your Advania account manager as soon as possible. ]]> Security architecture explained (part 6): Embedding security by design https://www.advania.co.uk/blog/security/security-architecture-explained-part-6-embedding-security-by-design/ Thu, 12 Feb 2026 09:11:06 +0000 https://advaniauk1stg.wpenginepowered.com/?post_type=insights&p=33168 It’s common for organisations to bolt on cyber defences with every new risk – but this hampers security in the long run. In this final entry of our security architecture series, we explain how embedding the principles of security by design can help progress your cyber strategy, and streamline your approach to protection.

Often, organisations end up with a “bolt-on” approach to their cyber strategy – deploying new defences ad hoc without understanding how they’ll integrate into the wider environment. This leads to a sprawling toolbox of cyber defences, which slows down security teams and can hamper their response to potential threats. In fast-growing environments, where new infrastructures and applications are widening the attack surface and further impeding the speed of response, there’s a law of diminishing returns for simply adding new tools to address the reality.

A more resilient approach is to adhere to the principle of security by design. We’ve touched on it throughout our security architecture series – as a cornerstone of security architecture, a vital consideration for governance, an applicable standard across different domains, a benefit of our Security Architecture-as-a-Service offering, and a core part of our approach. Now, in this entry, we’ll be outlining how you can embed these practices across your organisation as part of a unified, practical cyber strategy.

What security by design means

Security by design means integrating defence into the planning and delivery of systems from day one – ensuring that they’ve been built with a security-first outlook. It applies across the full lifecycle: from planning, to deployment, and into ongoing operations. The goal is to reduce vulnerabilities before they reach production, and to ensure cyber defences remain effective as services evolve.

This contrasts with more reactive approaches to security, where controls are added near the end of a project to satisfy a deadline, prepare for a compliance requirement, or simply because a new risk has elevated fear levels. In those scenarios, teams often face reworks, delays, and difficult compromises, which can cause security to come at the cost of other critical business objectives. Over time, this approach leads to inconsistencies in cyber defences, leaving gaps that attackers can exploit.

Security by design addresses this – by making cyber strategy a constant through every new application or infrastructure update, new capabilities can be built with existing cyber defences in mind, helping to rationalise the tools in your security stack. It also makes it straightforward to adhere to other key principles of effective security architecture, including a zero-trust approach to identity and access. Over time, these patterns help teams move faster, embedding security as part of everyday operations, rather than treating it as an additional hurdle.

Why security by design matters

Adopting the principles of security by design unlocks a more proactive cyber strategy – rather than responding to vulnerabilities as they’re exposed, new developments align with your security architecture. Common issues, like overly broad access permissions, misconfiguration, or monitoring blind spots – get addressed ahead of time, if they even crop up at all.

In a reactive approach, where these issues make it into production environments, remediation isn’t just disruptive, placing intense pressure on security teams, but often brings further expense to transformational projects. Applications require redesigning, deadlines need to be postponed for emergency changes, and, in some cases, the environment needs to be restored from earlier backups to avoid potential risks.

The benefits of a secure-by-design approach have given it significant momentum – especially in the public sector. Security by design is a core pillar of the UK government’s cyber security standard, with adherence to these principles being an essential part of the framework. It’s not hard to see why – in a high-stakes environment, with applications that store and process sensitive information, security by design mitigates the risk of cybercriminals gaining access.

Security by design also strengthens governance and compliance – with consistent, well-planned controls in place across IT environments, meeting regulatory requirements becomes much simpler. Plus, in the event of an audit, since all systems are part of a unified security architecture, finding the right documentation to demonstrate compliance is straightforward. This frees up valuable time for IT and security teams, who can approach compliance with confidence, rather than having to scramble and collect evidence of security when the time comes.

How we help you deliver

Adopting security by design is not just a matter of intent. It needs to be translated into practical decisions, repeatable patterns of behaviour, and a robust security architecture that can be applied across new and existing services. That is where a dedicated security architect plays a central role: bringing consistency to design choices, aligning delivery to frameworks and obligations, and ensuring cyber defences support the way the business actually operates.

Our team, working as part of a Security Architect-as-a-Service engagement, works with your teams – from security to application development – to embed the principles of security by design across your environment. We help you define requirements early, validate designs, and reduce gaps between what is expected in policy and what is implemented in practice, ensuring a consistent, unified approach. We also help rationalise cyber defences by aligning new capabilities with what is already in place, reducing duplication and avoiding tool sprawl that can slow responses and complicate day-to-day security operations.

Our flexible model makes that expertise easy to access, and in a way that suits the overarching needs of your business. Whether you need short-term input to implement security by design for a new project, or an ongoing partnership to develop your cyber strategy, our expertise is at your disposal.

Getting started with security by design

Embedding security design across your environment can seem a daunting task – especially if you’ve already grown into a broad suite of cyber defences through a bolt-on approach. But with the right expertise at your side, developing your cyber strategy can be straightforward, and adopting security by design makes it easier to iterate and improve on your defences often working with the tools already at your disposal.

Security by design works best when it’s translated into clear, repeatable decisions that teams can apply day to day. If you’d like a pragmatic view of where to start, what to prioritise, and how to reduce tools and complexity while strengthening your security architecture, we’re ready to help. Contact us to arrange an initial conversation and see how Security Architect-as-a-Service can support your plans.
