Advania is pleased to announce that Mirus IT Solutions is now fully operating under the Advania name, marking the next step in the continued integration of the Mirus team into the wider Advania UK organisation.

While the Mirus name is being retired, the people, expertise, and customer relationships behind it remain firmly in place.

Customers can expect the same high standards of service they are accustomed to, now delivered under the Advania brand.

Dan Sharp, Managing Director, SMB, comments: “This is an evolution rather than a change. The team our customers trust is the same, and so is our commitment to delivering reliable, high-quality IT support. What’s different is that our connection to Advania is now clear and visible.”

A long-standing relationship, made official
Mirus has been part of the Advania group for over five years, working closely with the wider business while continuing to operate day to day under its own name.

During that time, customers have benefited from the stability of the Mirus team alongside the backing of a larger technology organisation.

Aligning fully under the Advania name reflects how the business has already been operating behind the scenes. It also allows us to better represent the depth of capability available to Mirus customers, without changing the way they work with us.

“Operationally, we’ve been part of Advania for a long time,” Dan explains. “This step simply removes any ambiguity and makes it easier for customers to see the breadth of support and specialist expertise they can draw upon when needed.”

Access to broader expertise across the UK
As part of Advania, former Mirus customers gain clearer access to a nationwide technology partner delivering cloud services, cybersecurity, modern workplace solutions, data and AI, infrastructure, and managed IT services.

These capabilities complement and strengthen the existing Mirus offering, ensuring customers can scale their technology support as their needs evolve, without losing the personal service and trusted relationships they value.

What customer can expect
The Advania name has been adopted from 16 February 2026. From that date, customers will begin to see updated branding across communications, email addresses, and invoicing.

There are no changes to:

  • Existing contracts or pricing
  • Service levels or support arrangements
  • Day-to-day contacts or account teams

“Customers will continue working with the same people they know,” Dan adds.

“The difference is that those people are now clearly backed by Advania UK, and end to end technology organisation built for long-term partnership and growth.”

The insurance industry is undergoing a seismic shift. Once known for its paper-heavy processes and legacy systems, it’s now embracing digital transformation at rapid pace. At the heart of this evolution is InsurTech – technology designed to streamline, enhance, and future-proof insurance operations.

In this blog, we’ll be looking at how this transformation presents a unique opportunity to support insurers in modernising their infrastructure, improving compliance, and delivering better customer experiences.

What Is InsurTech?

InsurTech, short for insurance technology, refers to the use of innovative technologies to improve and disrupt the traditional insurance model. It spans everything from AI-powered underwriting tools to mobile-first customer portals and blockchain-based claims processing.

InsurTech aims to make the insurance model more efficient by using technology to better process claims, evaluate risk and underwrite policies. For example, it can use data from Internet of Things (IoT) devices to dynamically price premiums based on observed behaviour.

We’ve looked in more detail at how AI is reshaping the insurance industry, highlighting how it can set apart those firms that are adopting it effectively from those who are still reliant on legacy systems.  

While InsurTech is often associated with startups, it’s increasingly being adopted by established insurers looking to modernise their operations and stay competitive.

Recent innovations in InsurTech

The InsurTech landscape is rich with innovation. Some of the most impactful developments include:

  • Usage-based insurance (UBI): UBI allows insurers to leverage telematics and IoT to offer personalised premiums based on real-time behaviour, most commonly in car insurance.
  • Automated claims processing: AI can extract data from claims documents, detect fraud, and even assess damage through computer vision. This reduces the time and cost associated with manual claims processing and enhances customer satisfaction.
  • Chatbots and virtual assistants: Chatbots use natural language processing to understand and respond to queries, providing 24/7 support and faster query resolution.
  • Blockchain: This technology is enhancing transparency and security in insurance transactions. Blockchain can streamline the claims process, reduce fraud, and enable smart contracts that automatically execute when certain conditions are met.
  • Predictive analytics: Predictive analytics uses data to forecast future events and trends, helping insurers to better assess risk and personalise policies. This can predict claim likelihood, customer behaviour, and even potential fraud, enabling insurers to make more informed decisions.

Tech solutions to challenges in the insurance industry

Like many other sectors operating in the digital era, the insurance industry faces several challenges – but technology offers innovative solutions to address the most pressing issues.

Legacy systems

Many insurers still rely on outdated systems and infrastructure, which aren’t compatible with modern technology, making them difficult to integrate with modern tools. How can you overcome this? Migrating to cloud-based platforms and using APIs to integrate new technologies can modernise operations and improve efficiency.

Regulatory compliance

Navigating compliance regulations for insurers can be difficult and resource-intensive, even slowing down innovation. Regulatory technology can help you tackle this – it uses AI and machine learning to automate compliance processes, ensuring that insurers stay up to date with regulatory changes and reduce the risk of non-compliance.

Data privacy

With vast amounts of client data comes the responsibility of protecting it, especially with regulations like the GDPR in place. Data management in insurance is critical when your services rely on your data to improve product development, accurately match price to peril and make better underwriting decisions. Ensuring that you have robust data encryption and anonymisation techniques in place can help balance data privacy with the need for data-driven insights.

The role of data in InsurTech

Data plays a crucial role in modern insurance. From underwriting to claims, every touchpoint generates valuable insights. However, managing this data effectively is a challenge in itself.

One significant benefit of data in insurance is customer personalisation. By collecting and organizing personal information and demographic data, insurers can deliver tailored products and services that enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty.

Data management can also improve operational efficiency, with tools for data integration and business intelligence that can help consolidate data from multiple systems into a unified view.

Effective data management is fundamental to the success of modern insurance firms, enabling them to compete in today’s market by improving accuracy, efficiency, and customer satisfaction. Our guide on data management in insurance explores how insurers can harness data to drive smarter decisions, improve compliance, and enhance customer experiences.

How will technology change the insurance industry?

While InsurTech is already making waves, the journey is far from over. The next frontier will see greater efficiency, personalisation and security, driven by digital transformation in insurance. The integration of technology such as AI, blockchain, IoT and predictive analysis will transform how insurers assess risk and process claims. As the technology evolves, insurers will need to become more agile and responsive, offering tailored solutions that meet the dynamic needs of modern customers.

Technology is used to automate processes, personalise policies, improve risk assessment, and enhance customer service through tools like AI, IoT, and data analytics.

It’s enabling new business models, reducing operational costs, and shifting customer expectations toward digital-first experiences.

The Advania Group has published its 2024 Annual and Sustainability Report, marking a year of strong progress in climate action, governance and transparency for the six companies across Northern Europe. The group has chosen to align early with the EU’s Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) – well ahead of regulatory deadlines – demonstrating its commitment to clear, accountable, and forward-looking reporting.

“We report beyond what’s required because openness builds trust and drives performance. Sustainability isn’t a side effort – it’s a strategic priority for a fast-growing company with long-term ambitions.”
– Hege Støre, Advania Group CEO

Advancing circularity and climate action

A major milestone in 2024 was the launch of Advania Dreamhouse in Enköping, Sweden – a state-of-the-art refurbishment centre designed to process millions of IT devices annually. The facility is certified BREEAM-SE ‘Excellent’, underscoring Advania’s commitment to circularity, carbon reduction, and responsible growth.

In Sweden, Advania also achieved a 25% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, with 25% of hardware units sold coming from refurbished stock – boosting both environmental impact and customer value.

National highlights reflect local strengths

Advania’s decentralised model empowers each country to pursue sustainability priorities within a shared Group framework. 2024 highlights include:

  • Iceland: Launched Kolka, a digital platform that helps customers track, monitor, and manage their carbon footprint, supporting better sustainability decisions.
  • Norway: Introduced GHG reporting for IT services and product purchases, providing customers with deeper insight into the carbon footprint of their IT operations.
  • Denmark: Achieved 100% renewable energyusage across all data centre operations.
  • Finland: Joined a national public sector framework for responsible IT procurement, contributing to national carbon neutrality targets.
  • United Kingdom: Enhanced environmental data capturing initiatives and expanded local community engagement programs.
  • Sweden: Delivered strong results in circular IT through Dreamhouse and significantly reduced GHG emissions across operations.

Deepening trust, talent, and governance for long-term impact

With cyber security threats growing, Advania recognises that protecting sensitive data is a fundamental part of sustainable business. Investments made in 2024 strengthened digital resilience, safeguarded operations, and deepened customer trust – reinforcing cyber security as a key pillar of sustainability.

The year also saw continued expansion of Advania’s sustainable talent networks. In South Africa, Serbia, Sri Lanka and Poland, Advania’s nearshore hubs are built on selecting expertise, not cost. Salaries are based on role value rather than local wage levels, and community development is supported through donations of IT equipment to schools and partnerships with micro-businesses, including 100% black-owned businesses in South Africa.

Governance structures were further reinforced during the year. Advania introduced ESG-linked executive incentives, provided CSRD training for all country CEOs, and strengthened oversight through the Group Audit, Sustainability, Risk, and Compliance Committee. These measures reflect Advania’s ongoing commitment to embedding sustainability into every level of decision-making and leadership.
